About Me

Welcome! If you’ve already visited my home page, you know a bit about my academic background. Here, I’d like to share more about my journey as a Molecular Biophysics and Biochemistry student at Yale University, my passion for RNA biochemistry, and my commitment to global health initiatives.

I am deeply engaged in research aimed at understanding how RNA impacts human health, and I have a specific focus on discovering new ways to address health challenges in underserved communities. Through my academic work and global health efforts, I strive to make a meaningful impact on women's and children's health, particularly in Ethiopia.

When I’m not in the lab, I’m often involved in leadership activities, helping to mentor and guide younger students while advocating for diversity and inclusivity in the scientific community. I’m excited to continue exploring opportunities that allow me to merge my love for science and service.

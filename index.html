Genetics Targeting C/EBP Beta for Enhanced SMN2 Expression in Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA) Mouse Models

Organization: Yale University

class: BIOL 103: Genetics

Dates: Spring 2023

Overview

This project focuses on the therapeutic potential of overexpressing the transcription factor C/EBP Beta to enhance SMN2 expression in SMA mouse models. The project aims to investigate whether overexpression of C/EBP Beta can mitigate the severity of SMA by improving motor neuron survival and function.

Goal

The primary goal of this research is to determine the extent to which C/EBP Beta can upregulate SMN2 expression, leading to potential therapeutic approaches for Spinal Muscular Atrophy. The study seeks to enhance the production of full-length SMN proteins in motor neurons, which are critical in reducing SMA phenotypes.

Methodology

  • Cell Culture: Utilize iPSCs derived from SMA mouse models and healthy controls, differentiating them into motor neuron progenitor cells to study SMN2 expression.
  • C/EBP Beta Overexpression: Generate stable cell lines overexpressing C/EBP Beta using viral vectors, with GFP reporters for identification.
  • Quantitative Analysis: Measure SMN2 mRNA levels using qRT-PCR, followed by Western blotting to assess full-length SMN2 protein production.
  • Experimental Controls: Implement negative controls with empty vectors and positive controls with known SMN2 enhancers for comparison.
  • Statistical Analysis: Use appropriate statistical tests to evaluate the significance of changes in SMN2 expression across different experimental conditions.
  • Transgenic Mice Generation: Inject C/EBP Beta overexpression vectors into fertilized mouse oocytes and breed SMA-affected mice with transgenic offspring to assess improvements in motor function.
  • Phenotypic Analysis: Perform motor function tests such as grip strength and histological analysis of spinal cord sections to evaluate motor neuron survival in transgenic and control mice.
